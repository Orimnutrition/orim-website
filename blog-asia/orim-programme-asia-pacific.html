By Association ORIMMarch 202613 min readReviewed by nutrition professionals
The ORIM immunonutrition programme brings Swiss clinical precision to the Asia-Pacific region's most pressing health challenges. Developed by the Association ORIM in Geneva and built on decades of European immunonutrition research, the programme offers a structured, three-phase approach to immune optimization through eight scientifically formulated nutraceuticals. This article explains how each component addresses specific health priorities across the diverse Asia-Pacific region.

Why Asia-Pacific Needs Immunonutrition

The Asia-Pacific region faces a unique convergence of health challenges that immunonutrition is specifically designed to address. A diabetes and metabolic syndrome epidemic driven by rapid nutritional transition affects hundreds of millions. Widespread micronutrient deficiencies (vitamin D, iron, zinc, omega-3) undermine immune function across the region. Urban air pollution chronically damages respiratory and systemic immunity. High-stress work cultures suppress immune competence through cortisol dysregulation. Aging populations in Japan, South Korea, China, and Singapore face accelerating immunosenescence. Traditional dietary patterns that historically supported immune health are eroding under modernization pressures.

These challenges are interconnected through inflammatory and immune-metabolic pathways. Addressing them requires a comprehensive, multi-target approach rather than single-nutrient interventions. The ORIM programme was designed precisely for this complexity.

The ORIM Three-Phase Approach

Phase 1: Assessment and Foundation (Weeks 1-8)

The Foundation Phase corrects the nutritional deficiencies that undermine immune function. For Asia-Pacific populations, three products form the essential starting point:

  • Vitamin D + Zinc: Addresses the two most prevalent immune-critical deficiencies across the region. Vitamin D activates antimicrobial defenses and regulates adaptive immunity. Zinc supports thymic function and NK cell activity. Both are deficient across large populations from South Asia to Australia.
  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): Provides inflammation-resolving mediators (resolvins, protectins) critically lacking in Asian diets increasingly dominated by omega-6-rich vegetable oils. Pharmaceutical-grade purity ensures freedom from heavy metals relevant to Asian seafood concerns.
  • Probiotics: Restores gut-immune axis function with clinically studied Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium strains, addressing microbiome disruption from antibiotic overuse, processed food consumption, and stress, all prevalent across urban Asia.

Phase 2: Optimization (Weeks 8-16)

Once foundational deficiencies are corrected, the Optimization Phase adds targeted anti-inflammatory and immune-modulatory compounds:

  • Curcumin: Bioavailability-enhanced formulation targeting the NF-kB inflammatory pathway central to metabolic syndrome, pollution-related inflammation, and stress-driven immune suppression. Builds on Asia's turmeric traditions with clinically effective dosing.
  • Polyphenols: Multi-source antioxidant complex providing broad-spectrum protection against the oxidative stress generated by pollution, UV exposure, and metabolic inflammation across the region.
  • Postbiotics: Next-generation gut-immune support delivering bacterial metabolites (SCFAs, bacteriocins) that maintain gut barrier function and immune regulation even when live probiotic colonization is challenging.

Phase 3: Maintenance (Ongoing)

The Maintenance Phase sustains optimal immune function with continued foundational support and two additional formulations:

  • Spirulina + Chlorella: Microalgae complex providing detoxification support (relevant to pollution-exposed populations), iron and B12 (relevant to vegetarian populations across South Asia), and unique immune-stimulating phycocyanins.
  • Multivitamin Immune: Comprehensive formula addressing the full spectrum of immune-relevant micronutrients, ensuring no critical gaps regardless of individual dietary patterns across the diverse region.

Getting Started with ORIM in Asia-Pacific

  1. Assess: Test your vitamin D level, inflammatory markers (CRP), and omega-3 index through your local healthcare provider or laboratory.
  2. Begin Foundation Phase: Start with vitamin D + zinc, omega-3, and probiotics for 8 weeks to correct foundational deficiencies.
  3. Add Optimization: Introduce curcumin, polyphenols, and postbiotics for targeted anti-inflammatory and immune-modulatory support.
  4. Maintain: Continue the full programme with spirulina/chlorella and multivitamin immune for comprehensive, ongoing immune optimization.
  5. Monitor: Retest biomarkers at 3-6 month intervals to track improvement and adjust as needed.
