ORIM WEEKLY

The weekly letter on preventive immunonutrition

WEEK

September 4-10

EDITORIAL

Early September brings a new rhythm as routines resume after summer. The shorter days trigger metabolic shifts toward energy storage and immune preparation. This week explores how autumn's first foods support this biological transition.

01

SMARTFARMING & AGRITECH

September Light and Immune Gene Expression Shifts

Decreasing photoperiod in September activates a cascade of immune gene expression changes. A 2022 study in Nature Communications showed that over 4,000 genes display seasonal expression patterns, with September marking the transition from anti-inflammatory summer patterns to the pro-inflammatory winter phenotype. This shift increases pathogen vigilance but risks excessive inflammation without nutritional support.

Mushroom Beta-Glucans and Trained Immunity

Autumn mushroom season provides access to beta-glucans that induce trained immunity in monocytes. Research in the Journal of Leukocyte Biology (2023) demonstrated that shiitake, maitake, and oyster mushroom beta-glucans reprogrammed monocyte epigenetics within three days of consumption, enhancing their pathogen responsiveness for up to three months, perfectly timed for winter immune preparation.

Forest-to-Table: Wild Mushroom Sustainability

Sustainable wild mushroom foraging is gaining AgriTech support through AI identification apps. A 2023 study in Mycological Research showed that responsibly harvested wild mushrooms contained 35% more ergothioneine and 50% more vitamin D2 than cultivated varieties, while proper harvesting techniques preserved mycelial networks for future production.

02

BIOLOGICAL ADVANCES

Shiitake Mushrooms: Autumn's Immune Trainer

Lentinula edodes produces lentinan, a beta-1,3-glucan approved as an adjunct immunotherapy in Japan. A 2022 clinical trial showed that daily shiitake consumption for four weeks increased IgA secretion by 25% and improved gamma-delta T-cell function. These immune enhancements persisted for eight weeks after cessation, demonstrating true trained immunity induction.

Dry shiitake mushrooms in sunlight for 30 minutes before use. UV exposure converts ergosterol to vitamin D2, increasing their vitamin D content by up to 1000% while intensifying umami flavor.

THIS WEEK IN BRIEF

► Swiss mycological societies report excellent early autumn mushroom season following wet August conditions.

► AI mushroom identification apps reach 95% accuracy, enabling safe wild foraging for nutritional purposes.

► Japanese immunotherapy protocols using lentinan inspire European research into food-based immune training.
