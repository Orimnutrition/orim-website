ORIM WEEKLY

The weekly letter on preventive immunonutrition

WEEK

August 12-18

EDITORIAL

Mid-August is the traditional period of Perseids meteor showers and late summer abundance. This week we explore the anti-inflammatory properties of omega-3-rich oily fish at their peak summer condition. Our chronobiology segment reveals how stargazing darkness promotes melatonin and immune repair.

01

SMARTFARMING & AGRITECH

Omega-3 Resolution Pathways: Beyond Anti-Inflammation

EPA and DHA are precursors to specialised pro-resolving mediators (SPMs): resolvins, protectins, and maresins. A 2023 review in the Journal of Clinical Investigation showed that SPMs actively resolve inflammation rather than merely suppressing it, clearing cellular debris and restoring tissue homeostasis. An omega-3 index above 8% ensures adequate SPM production capacity.

Darkness Exposure and Melatonin Amplitude

True darkness (less than 1 lux) for 8 continuous hours increases melatonin peak amplitude by 50% compared to dim-light conditions (5-10 lux). A 2024 study in PNAS showed that just one night of complete darkness reset suppressed melatonin rhythms within 48 hours. Summer stargazing away from light pollution provides both melatonin rescue and circadian re-entrainment.

Seaweed Biorefinery and Functional Food Ingredients

Integrated seaweed biorefineries extract fucoidan, alginate, and omega-3 DHA from brown macroalgae with 90% material utilisation. A 2023 paper in Trends in Biotechnology showed that fucoidan activates NK cells with potency comparable to beta-glucan supplements. Marine biomass offers a sustainable, land-free source of immune-modulating polysaccharides and essential fatty acids.

02

BIOLOGICAL ADVANCES

Sardines: Omega-3 and Calcium Superfood

A 100 g serving of sardines provides 1.5 g EPA+DHA, 382 mg calcium (from edible bones), 5 mcg vitamin D, and 48 mcg selenium. This single food addresses four of the most common nutritional deficiencies simultaneously. August sardines from cold Atlantic waters contain peak fat content, making late summer the ideal season for this nutritional powerhouse.

Sustainability: Sardines are among the most sustainable seafood choices. Their short lifespan means minimal mercury bioaccumulation.
