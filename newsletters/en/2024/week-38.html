ORIM WEEKLY

The weekly letter on preventive immunonutrition

WEEK

September 16-22

EDITORIAL

The autumnal equinox arrives this week, bringing equal day and night. This pivotal astronomical moment triggers profound immune reprogramming as the body shifts from summer's UV-defence mode to winter's pathogen-defence mode. Nutrition during this transition week sets the immune tone for the coming cold season.

01

SMARTFARMING & AGRITECH

Equinox Immune Reprogramming: TH2 to TH1 Shift

The autumn equinox triggers a gradual shift from summer's TH2-dominant allergic responses toward TH1-mediated antiviral immunity. A 2023 study in Nature Immunology analysed 10,000 blood samples and found that interferon-gamma-producing CD4+ cells increase by 25% between September and November. Zinc and vitamin D supplementation accelerate this protective seasonal transition.

Equinox Sleep Architecture and Immune Cell Distribution

The 12:12 light-dark ratio of the equinox produces the most balanced sleep architecture of the year, with equal proportions of REM and deep sleep. A 2024 study in Sleep Medicine showed that equinox-period sleep produced the highest concentration of tissue-regenerative growth hormone pulses. This makes equinox week the ideal time for immune system recovery and recalibration.

Root Cellar Storage and Long-Term Nutrient Preservation

Traditional root cellar storage at 0-4 degrees Celsius and 90-95% humidity preserves root vegetable vitamin C for 4-6 months with only 10-15% losses. A 2023 study in Postharvest Biology and Technology showed that cellar-stored carrots retained 85% of beta-carotene after 5 months versus 60% for refrigerator storage. Ancient food preservation outperforms modern cold chains.

02

BIOLOGICAL ADVANCES

Pumpkin: Immune Transition Superfood

Pumpkin provides 245% of daily vitamin A as beta-carotene per cup, alongside 2 mg zinc per serving. Its seeds add 7.6 mg zinc per 100 g. This dual flesh-and-seed nutrition profile uniquely supports the autumn TH1 shift, as both retinol and zinc are required cofactors for interferon-gamma production. September pumpkins at peak ripeness offer maximum carotenoid density.

Storage: Whole pumpkins last 3-6 months in a cool, dry place. Once cut, use within 5 days and store seeds for roasting.
